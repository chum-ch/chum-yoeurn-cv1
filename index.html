Neak loeung bridge

Neak Leoung bridge Mekong in Cambodia: Neak Loeung/Tsubasa bridge (Neak Loeung/Tsubasa bridge) is a bridge across the Mekong built with Japanese aid and praised by Cambodians as "our Golden Gate Bridge". The construction of this project started on February 14, 2011, with a total budget of 131 million U.S. dollars, with approximately 92 million U.S. dollars in free aid from the Japanese government and more than 2.9 million U.S. dollars from the Cambodian government. The total length of the bridge is 2215 meters and the double-ended road connecting the bridge is long. 3100 meters, of which the west bank road is 890 meters long and the east bank road is 2210 meters long, and the road is 14 meters wide. For the moment, the Haliang Bridge will become the longest bridge in Cambodia, 300 meters longer than the 1900-meter bridge in Kuo Kung Province.

Ba Phnom tourism

Ba Phnom was one of the first religious and cultural sites in Cambodia and may be considered a birthplace of the Cambodian nation. It was founded in the 5th century and was used as a place of pilgrimage for various kings until the 19th century. Human sacrifices were also reported to take place here until the 19th century. Despite its history there is little that remains at Ba Phnom. What remains is a modern wat, some small pagodas, and the rebuilt ruins of Preah Vihear Chann, which was an 11th century temple. Most of the temple was destroyed, so the rebuilt temple is mostly concrete with a corrugated roof, and is the place where it assumes that there was a king capital Nokor Phnom.
